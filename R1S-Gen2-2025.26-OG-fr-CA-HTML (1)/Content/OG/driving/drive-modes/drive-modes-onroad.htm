On-Road Drive Modes

Icon

Drive Mode

Description

All‑Purpose

All-Purpose mode maximizes comfort for daily driving. As the most versatile mode, All‑Purpose is recommended in all on‑road conditions, including rainy weather. This mode keeps the vehicle at Standard ride height for city driving, and changes to Low ride height at highway speeds for better aerodynamics and increased range. See Ride Height to customize this setting.

  • In Dual‑Motor vehicles, All‑Purpose mode automatically switches to front‑wheel drive when conditions allow to save energy and maximize range.

  • In Dual-Motor vehicles, the drive mode resets to All-Purpose after 4 hours since your last drive.

  • In Tri-Motor and Quad-Motor vehicles, the drive mode resets to All‑Purpose after 4 hours since your last drive if you were in an off‑road drive mode.

Sport

Sport mode prioritizes on‑road vehicle performance and a responsive driving experience. Sport mode stiffens and lowers the suspension for optimal responsiveness and handling. The vehicle stays in all‑wheel drive for maximum power transfer between tires and pavement.

Conserve (Tri‑Motor and Quad‑Motor)

Conserve mode automatically switches between front‑wheel drive and all‑wheel drive when conditions allow to save energy and maximize range.

Snow

Snow mode maximizes confidence and control on roads and highways with snow, slush, or ice. A relaxed pedal response and reduced regenerative braking allow for more precise control on low‑grip surfaces. Like All‑Purpose, this mode keeps the vehicle at Standard ride height for city driving, and changes to Low ride height at highway speeds. See Ride Height to customize this setting.

To go off road in snow, use All‑Terrain mode.
