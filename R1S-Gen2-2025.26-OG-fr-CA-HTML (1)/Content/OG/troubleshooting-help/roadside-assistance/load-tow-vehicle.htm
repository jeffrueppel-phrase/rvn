Load the Vehicle onto the Tow Truck

After you've connected the vehicle, you can load the vehicle onto a flatbed tow truck as follows:

  1. Ensure the Park brake release is turned on or the rear wheels are lifted. See Prepare the Vehicle for Towing.

  2. Remove the wheel chocks, if needed.

  3. Use the tow truck winch to pull the vehicle onto the tow truck bed.

  4. Pull at or less to prevent damage to the powertrain.

    If you pull too fast, the vehicle stops and shifts to P (Park).

  5. Tie down the vehicle using eight-point retention straps with two points of contact per corner.

    To prevent damage, ensure the straps don't contact the brake lines or suspension components behind the tire.

  6. Put the vehicle into P (Park).

  7. Go to Drive Modes  in the center display. Choose All‑Purpose , and then choose Low under Ride Height.

  8. If you're transporting the vehicle over , turn on Vehicle Shipping Mode to preserve battery charge.
