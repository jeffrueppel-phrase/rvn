  1. Go to Drive Modes  > All-Terrain  (under Off-Road on Quad- and Tri‑Motor vehicles). Then choose Highest under Ride Height.

  2. After the suspension has finished adjusting to Highest, go to Settings  > Vehicle > Service. Then turn on Tire Change mode.

    When Tire Change mode is on, automatic suspension adjustments pause and ride height can't be changed.

    1. Remove the wheel chocks from the storage tray.

    2. Secure the vehicle by placing wheel chocks diagonally opposite the tire you're changing.

      The electronic park brake is only on the rear wheels.
      If you lift a rear wheel, the vehicle may roll.

    1. Locate the vehicle jack point closest to the tire you're changing. There is a jack point behind each front tire and in front of each rear tire, near the rocker panel.

      To avoid damage to the vehicle and injury to people nearby, only place the tire jack under the vehicle jack point. Don't place the tire jack under other locations.

    2. Remove the jack point cover by applying force to the inner edge.

    3. Remove the tire jack and handle from the storage kit.

    4. Position the tire jack under the jack point.

    5. Align the top of the tire jack with the hole at the vehicle jack point.

      Front

      Rear

    To avoid damage to the vehicle and injury to people nearby, ensure that the jack is straight and the pin at the top of the tire jack is level and aligned with the opening in the vehicle jack point. The jack may slip if not aligned and level.

    1. Insert the jack handle into the tire jack. Then turn the handle to raise the jack slightly.

    2. If the jack begins to tilt, lower the jack. Then reposition the jack rearward approximately and try again.

    3. Continue to Remove and Replace a Wheel.
