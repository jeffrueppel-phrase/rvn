Lift the Rear Wheels

If the battery is dead or you can't release the electronic park brake, lift the rear wheels to load the vehicle onto a tow truck.

Secure the vehicle and connect it to the tow truck before you lift the rear wheels. The vehicle can roll when you lift the rear wheels.

Only lift the rear wheels to load the vehicle onto the tow truck. Don't tow the vehicle behind another vehicle or with two wheels on the ground.

  1. Chock the front wheels to secure the vehicle.

  2. Connect the vehicle to the tow truck using an endless loop through the front control arms or rear trailer hitch.

  3. Lift the rear wheels using skates, a dolly, or a tow truck wheel lift.

  4. With the rear wheels off the ground, position the vehicle to be loaded onto the tow truck bed.

    You can now load the vehicle onto the tow truck.

    If using skates, use the skates to pull the vehicle all the way up the tow truck ramp.
